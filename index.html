Project Plan/Description

App Name: Gaze - We are Looking!

The application that our group has chosen to work on is a mobile application (app) named “Gaze”. The app is a platform where users can report emergency incidents or crime.

After considerable deliberation, we validated that there was no application on the market that allowed users to report any incidents with a few clicks, which is a completely untouchable market segment. In the early stages of idea development and project development, the tea agreed on what least viable features, as well as some advanced features that were considered "desirable".

At a minimum, the Gaze mobile application will be able to notify users of the video, audio, or image input feature of a crime / emergency. The goal of our group was to make the experience as smooth as possible and to provide the user with a full-fledged experience that includes fast features, suggestions, and easy search. When using the system, the user will have the option to login, sign up or they can avoid this step, which will give them full control over the app. There are no restrictions when reporting an incident because they can hide their identity if they do not feel safe or comfortable. The process of reporting on the app is designed to be intuitive, users can add audio, video, or photo about the incident.

Our project concept is based on developing an app for emergency services reporting that includes a few simple steps for easy and quick reporting. The app's main purpose is to be easily accessible and to provide firsthand information to the end user in the shortest amount of time. Because of its simple and easy-to-use interface, it will appeal to a wide range of age groups, from children to the elderly. We believe it can be more effective than traditional methods such as phone reporting or, in some cases, reporting an incident or crime on a website, which can be time-consuming and complex.

According to our research, the app's strength lies in its uniqueness and design; no such apps are available in the market. It has the potential to completely transform the way we report crime or incidents. According to one study, the average person spends five to six hours per day using their mobile phone, and another study shows that people use social apps to chat to friends versus calling friends for two hours. Based on this trend, there is a strong probability that people will report crime/incident on app as opposed to traditional methods of calling or web browser based online reporting.

Aim and Goal

Aim

To develop an app to make society a better place by enabling layman report crimes and emergency incidents effortlessly without putting themselves in danger and help the concerned departments work more efficiently minimising chances of loss of lives and other damages

Goal

The main goal of the ‘Gaze’ is to provide a quick and easy solution to report an incident or crime with help of few clicks in a reliable app. The app will allow users to report any emergency or incident in seconds with just four quick taps on their smartphone (IOS or Android). It will be distinct from other conventional methods of contacting emergency departments as it will provide evidence of the incident on the spot in the form of image, video, or audio clip. It can provide accurate and detailed information about the situation.

In some cases, calling the cops can land the person in a trouble. Making a video, taking picture, or recording audio clip would reduce the risk of being overheard by the culprit. The location can be automatically shared which will save time and damage can be minimised. It will have a very user-friendly and simple interface so that the user does not get bogged down in complexities. This app is going to be different as it can be used to report all types of incidents in one app unlike Emergency+, Fires near me etc. It will aid in providing first-hand visual or audio for the best response and response time.

It will work in online mode (photos and videos included) or offline mode (no video or photo included, only for quick sending, i.e., the text only report) in areas with limited signals. It will also have the option of hiding the identity of the person reporting the crime, as many crimes go unreported due to fear of identity disclosure. They don't even have to leave the house to report it. Which are some of the most common reasons why people do not report crime.
